01

What Claude Code actually is (and isn't)

First, let's fix the mental picture — because it's the thing most people get wrong.

You've probably used ChatGPT or the Claude chat app: you type a question, it types an answer. Useful, but it lives in a little box — it can't see your inventory sheet, it forgets you tomorrow, and it can't do anything.

Claude Code is different. Think of it less like a search box and more like a sharp, tireless assistant who sits at a desk inside your shop's folder on your computer. You point it at a folder full of your business stuff, and from then on it can:

📊

Read your real files

Open your Excel inventory and sales sheets and answer with your actual numbers — not guesses.

✍️

Do real work

Write a week of Facebook posts, build a price list, draft a supplier contract, make a PDF invoice.

🧠

Remember your business

Once you tell it about your two shops, it keeps that in mind every time — no re-explaining.

🔌

Connect your tools

Optionally plug in Notion, Google Drive, or web research so it works with the tools you already use.

💡
The one big idea

You're not "using an AI tool." You're hiring a partner and teaching it your business once. The 30 minutes you spend setting it up is you training a teammate who then shows up every day, never forgets, and works for the price of a couple of coffees a month.

Yes — it's called "Code" and developers love it for programming. But underneath, it's a general-purpose assistant that's great with files, documents, and numbers. You will not need to write any code. Ever. Promise.

02

⚡ Quick start — get talking in ~30 minutes

Four steps. Do them in order. By the end you'll be chatting with a partner who can see your files.

1

Get a Claude Pro plan ($20/month)

Claude Code needs a paid plan — the free plan does not include it. The cheapest one that does is Claude Pro at $20/month, and it covers both the normal Claude chat app and Claude Code under one login.

  • Go to claude.com in your web browser and sign up / subscribe to Pro.
  • Your Philippine Visa or Mastercard works — you'll be billed in US dollars (~₱1,200/month depending on the exchange rate).
  • Subscribe on the website, not through a phone app store — app-store prices can be higher.
🗓️
Later, if you outgrow it

Heavier use? Max is $100/mo (5× the usage) or $200/mo (20×). You almost certainly won't need it starting out. Stay on Pro.

2

Install it on Windows

You've got two ways. Since you're comfortable installing apps, I recommend the Desktop app — it's a normal window, no black command screen.

🟢 The easy way — the Desktop app (recommended for you)

  1. Install Git first. On Windows, the Desktop app needs it. Download from git-scm.com/downloads/win, run the installer, and just click Next through it (the defaults are fine).
  2. Go to claude.com/download and download the Windows installer, then run it.
  3. Launch Claude from your Start menu and sign in with the account you made in Step 1.
  4. Click the “Code” tab at the top, choose “Local”, then “Select folder” (you'll make that folder in Step 3).

⚫ The other way — the command line (one line, if you prefer)

If you'd rather use the terminal: open PowerShell (search "PowerShell" in the Start menu) and paste this one line, then press Enter:

Paste into PowerShell (not Command Prompt)
irm https://claude.ai/install.ps1 | iex

When it finishes, close and reopen PowerShell, then check it worked:

Check the install
claude --version
⚠️
PowerShell vs Command Prompt

That irm ... | iex line only works in PowerShell (the prompt starts with PS C:\). If you see an error about 'irm' is not recognized, you're in the plain Command Prompt — open PowerShell instead. Easy trap; now you know it.

3

Make a “business brain” folder

Claude Code works inside one folder at a time — think of a folder as a “project.” Make one home for everything about your shops. For example, in your Documents, create:

A simple folder layout
My Business/
   perfume/
      inventory.xlsx
      sales.xlsx
   vape/
      inventory.xlsx
      sales.xlsx
   accounting.xlsx

Drop copies of your POS exports and Excel sheets in there. In the Desktop app, click “Select folder” and choose My Business. That's it — Claude can now see those files.

✅
Why copies, not originals

Put copies in the folder while you're learning, so your live POS files are never at risk. Later, when you trust it, you can point it at the real ones.

🧠 The one habit that matters: give it a memory

This single file is what turns Claude Code from “a smart tool” into “a partner that knows my business.”

Every time Claude Code starts up in your folder, it automatically reads a plain text file called CLAUDE.md — your briefing sheet. Whatever you write in it, Claude knows before you say a word: your shops, where your files are, how you like things done, your rules.

Write it once, and you stop re-explaining yourself forever. This is the highest-value 10 minutes in this whole guide.

Two easy ways to create it

  • Let Claude do it: type /init and it will look at your folder and write a starter CLAUDE.md for you.
  • Or paste mine: copy the template below, save it as CLAUDE.md inside your My Business folder, and fill in the [brackets].
📄 CLAUDE.md — starter for a perfume + vape owner (edit the brackets)
# CLAUDE.md — About My Businesses

## Who I am
- I'm [YOUR NAME], owner of two retail shops in [City], Philippines.
- I run them with a small team. I'm not a programmer.
- Talk to me in plain, simple English. Explain like I'm smart but new to this.
- Money is in Philippine Pesos. Write dates as DD/MM/YYYY.

## My two businesses — keep them SEPARATE unless I ask to compare
### 1. [Perfume Shop Name] — perfumes & fragrances
- We sell [inspired/authentic] perfumes, oils, testers and decants.
- Best sellers: [name a few]. Price range: about P[___] to P[___].
- Main suppliers: [names]. We reorder about every [2 weeks].

### 2. [Vape Shop Name] — vape products (18+ only)
- We sell [devices, pods, e-liquids, coils, accessories].
- This is an age-restricted product. Keep ALL marketing responsible,
  18+ only, no health claims.
- Best sellers: [name a few]. Main suppliers: [names].

## Where my files are (inside this folder)
- perfume/inventory.xlsx  — stock, quantities, cost price, selling price
- perfume/sales.xlsx      — sales exported from the POS
- vape/inventory.xlsx and vape/sales.xlsx — same, for the vape shop
- accounting.xlsx         — expenses, income, running totals

## How I want you to help
- When I ask about stock or money, READ the Excel files first,
  then answer with real numbers and show where they came from.
- Before you change any Excel file, save a copy first
  (like inventory_BACKUP.xlsx) and tell me you did.
- Facebook posts: friendly and local, never spammy.
  Perfume = sensory and aspirational. Vape = responsible, 18+.
- If something could cost me money or lose data, STOP and ask me first.

## Things to remember
- (You can add notes here over time, or I'll say "remember that...")
🗣️
Two ways to update its memory later

You never have to hand-edit the file if you don't want to. Just tell Claude “remember that our new supplier is X” and it saves the note itself. To see or edit everything it remembers, type /memory.

🚫
Ignore old advice about the “#” key

Older tutorials say “press # to save a memory.” That shortcut was removed — it doesn't work anymore. Use /memory or just say “remember that…” instead.

A tool you have to re-explain every morning isn't a partner. The briefing sheet is what makes it one.

🔌 Connectors — plugging in your other tools

What they are, which ones actually matter for your shops, and the honest truth about Facebook.

A connector lets Claude reach into another app you use — like Notion or Google Drive — and read or update things there. The technical name is “MCP,” but you can forget that word. Think: plug-ins.

🎉
Great news — the big one needs zero setup

Reading and writing your Excel files and folders is built in. No connector needed for your inventory, sales, or accounting sheets. Web research is built in too — you can ask it to look up suppliers or competitor prices out of the box.

The easiest way to add a connector

You don't touch the terminal. In the Claude app, go to Customize → Connectors (or click the + in a chat and hover “Connectors”), find the one you want, toggle it on, and sign in through your browser. Once it's on there, it automatically shows up in Claude Code too, because it's the same account.

📌
The honest truth about Facebook

There's no simple, official one-click connector for posting to a Facebook Page yet. (Meta's official tool is for paid ads only.) Auto-posting is possible but it's an advanced, techie setup. My advice: have Claude write your posts (it's brilliant at that), then you copy-paste them into Facebook yourself. Takes 20 seconds and you stay in control of what goes live.

🧹
Rule of thumb

Only switch on connectors you actually use. Each one takes up a little room in Claude's “memory” every session, so a tidy setup is a faster, cheaper one.

05

🛠️ Skills — teaching it special talents (incl. contracts)

Skills are saved abilities. We'll turn on the document pack, then give you a custom contract-maker.

A skill is a saved instruction sheet that gives Claude a repeatable talent — like “make documents” or “draft my contracts the way I like.” Claude either uses a skill automatically when it fits your request, or you trigger it by typing a slash and its name (e.g. /business-docs).

Step 1 — Install the document pack (Word, Excel, PDF, PowerPoint)

This turns Claude Code into a document maker — invoices, price lists, reports, flyers, decks. Here's the one gotcha most people miss: in Claude Code these are NOT on by default (they're automatic only in the chat app). You install them once. Type these inside Claude Code, one at a time:

Type inside Claude Code — 1) add the marketplace
/plugin marketplace add anthropics/skills
2) install the document skills
/plugin install document-skills@anthropic-agent-skills
3) turn them on now
/reload-plugins
🧩
Note the spelling

You add the marketplace as anthropics/skills, but it installs from a name called anthropic-agent-skills — that's why the second command ends in @anthropic-agent-skills. If a command says “not found,” you probably mistyped that part.

Step 2 — Add your own “Contracts & Business Docs” skill

This is the one you asked about — a contract maker built for a retail shop, not a law firm. It knows how to draft supplier agreements, consignment deals, staff contracts, invoices, receipts, and collection letters, and it asks you for the details it needs. It's yours to keep and edit.

How to install it: make a new folder on your PC at C:\Users\YOUR-NAME\.claude\skills\business-docs\ then save the text below inside it as a file named SKILL.md. (Or just paste the whole block to Claude and say “save this as my business-docs skill.”) Then run /reload-plugins.

📄 SKILL.md — your retail contract & document maker
---
name: business-docs
description: Drafts retail business documents for a small shop owner —
  supplier agreements, consignment agreements, staff employment contracts,
  sales invoices, official-style receipts, price lists, and collection or
  demand letters. Use whenever the user asks to draft, write, make, or
  generate any contract, agreement, invoice, receipt, quotation, or
  formal business letter.
---

# Business Documents (Retail)

Help a non-lawyer shop owner produce clean, professional documents from a
short brief. Keep the language plain and firm. Currency is Philippine Pesos
unless told otherwise.

## Step 1 — Gather the essentials (ask, don't assume)
Before writing, check the document type below and ask — in ONE short list —
for any missing must-have fields. If the owner says "just use placeholders,"
fill them with clearly marked [BRACKETS].

## Step 2 — Pick the document type and follow its checklist

### Supplier / Purchase Agreement
Need: supplier name & address, shop name & address, products supplied,
prices & payment terms, delivery lead time, order minimums, start date &
duration, who handles defects/returns.
Sections: Parties · Products & Prices · Orders & Delivery · Payment ·
Returns & Defects · Term & Termination · Confidentiality · Signatures.

### Consignment Agreement (goods placed on consignment)
Need: consignor (owner of goods), consignee (the shop), product list &
agreed retail prices, commission/split %, settlement schedule, who bears
loss or theft, return of unsold goods.
Sections: Parties · Consigned Goods & Prices · Commission & Settlement ·
Risk & Insurance · Reporting · Return of Goods · Term · Signatures.

### Staff / Employment Contract
Need: employee name, position, start date, location, schedule, salary &
pay dates, probation period, duties, confidentiality, grounds for ending.
Sections: Parties · Position & Duties · Pay & Benefits · Working Hours ·
Probation · Confidentiality & Company Property · Termination · Signatures.
(PH basics to respect: probation is max 6 months; observe minimum wage and
13th-month pay.)

### Sales Invoice / Quotation
Need: shop name & contact, customer, date, number, line items
(description, qty, unit price, amount), subtotal, discount, total,
payment method/terms.
Layout: Header (room for a logo) · Bill-To · Items table · Totals ·
Payment terms · Thank-you line.

### Official-style Receipt
Need: shop name & contact, receipt number, date, customer, items, amount
paid, payment method, "Received the amount of ...", signature line.

### Collection / Demand Letter
Need: debtor name, amount owed, original due date, what it was for, new
deadline, and tone (friendly reminder vs firm final notice).
Flow: Date · Recipient · Subject · Facts → amount → deadline → consequence
· Closing.

## Step 3 — Write it
- Produce the full document as clean, ready-to-paste text.
- Use headings, numbered clauses, and a signature block with
  name / signature / date lines for each party.
- Short, readable clauses. No unnecessary legalese.

## Step 4 — Offer to export
Offer to save it as a Word (.docx) or PDF. If the user agrees and the
document-skills plugin is installed, use the docx or pdf skill to make
the file.

## Always include this note
End every contract or agreement with: "This is a starting draft, not legal
advice. For anything valuable or long-term, have a licensed lawyer review it
before signing." Never claim a document is legally guaranteed or notarized.

✅ Habits that make you good at this

Five small habits, plus the only commands you need to memorize.

  1. Be specific. “Make a report” is weak. “Make a one-page monthly sales report grouped by product, highest first” gets gold. Give it the details you'd give a new employee.
  2. Iterate, don't restart. If the first answer isn't right, say “make it shorter” or “more casual” — it builds on what it just did. It's a conversation.
  3. Ask it to think first. For anything big, press Shift+Tab to switch to Plan mode — it explains its plan without changing any files until you're happy.
  4. Start fresh for new topics. Finished with inventory and moving to marketing? Type /clear so it isn't dragging old context around. Your files are untouched.
  5. Let it remember. Whenever you correct it (“we always price in round numbers”), add “— remember that.” Next time it just knows.

08

🔒 Safety & money — read this once, relax forever

Nothing scary here — just the handful of things that keep your files and your wallet safe.

You're always in control

Claude Code can't secretly change anything. Reading files is free and automatic, but before it edits a file or runs a command, it shows you exactly what it wants to do and waits for your “Yes.” When in doubt, use Plan mode (Shift+Tab) so it only advises.

💾
Back up your money files

Keep your Excel sheets in a folder that syncs to Google Drive or OneDrive, and work on copies. There's a /rewind command to undo Claude's edits — but it only undoes edits Claude made with its own tools, not files removed by a system command. So /rewind is a nice safety net, not a substitute for real backups.

💳
Two buttons that cost extra — leave them OFF

Your $20 Pro plan is a flat fee. Two things can add charges on top: the /fast mode, and any prompt to “enable usage credits.” As a beginner, say no to both and you'll never get a surprise bill. Check spending anytime with /cost.

🤫
Keep secrets secret

Don't paste passwords, full card numbers, or sensitive customer data unless you truly need to. Treat it like a trusted staff member — helpful and private, but you still don't hand over the keys to everything.

🧠
It can be confidently wrong

It's brilliant but not perfect. Always sanity-check numbers before you act on them, and for anything legal, tax, or high-value (like a big contract), have a real professional review it. The contract skill even reminds you of this in every document it makes.
